I first got into programming when I was in highschool. Like most schools, we had to do a senior project and I chose learning JavaScript! That summer after I graduated highschool, I used that project to get an internship at a company in Los Angeles called The Rubicon Project.

At Rubicon, I got to work on their big data team and learned a lot! Eventually, another intern and I were tasked with creating a web app that would display the companies load balancer information from their data centers all over the world. Previously, that information was near impossible to find unless you were an active developer. We made that information easy for everyone to see!

After my time at Rubicon, I worked in a floor covering warehouse in the NW Industial Park in Portland loading and unloading trucks as well as cutting carpet and other flooring for installers to take the next day for instulation all over the greater Portland Metro Area! I worked that job while attending Clark College for Computer Science.

After some time at Clark and doing little to no relevant programming, I decided that I wanted to take a more direct approach to becoming a Software Developer and I enrolled at Epicodus!
